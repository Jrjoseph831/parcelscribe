## What you need
- Tracking number and expected delivery date
- Proof of shipment (label PDF, drop-off receipt, manifest)
- Proof of value (invoice/receipt)
- Recipient contact and address

## Steps
1. Collect proof of shipment and value.
2. Summarize tracking with last scan and expected delivery date.
3. Submit the claim; include recipient contact info.
4. Follow up weekly and respond quickly to UPS info requests.
5. If found or delivered later, update UPS; if denied, appeal with clearer proof.

## Common mistakes
- Waiting too long to file while scans stall
- No proof of shipment or value attached
- Leaving out delivery address and recipient contact
- Not including a concise tracking timeline

## What to upload
- Proof of shipment (label/receipt)
- Proof of value
- Tracking timeline with last scan

## Example claim narrative
"Tracking 1ZXXXX stopped scanning on 01/02; expected delivery 01/04. Proof of shipment and value attached. Recipient contact included. Please investigate and reimburse the contents value."
